Get To Know Me A Little

I have been interested in computers ever since I saw one of my cousins play fifa on his play station 2. I couldn't get it out of my mind. I knew I had to beg my parents to get me one. So beg I did. Couple of years went by and finally they bought it for me. To say I was happy would be an understatement. I did not sleep that night and subsequent nights. The game had taken hold of me. Every now and then when I got a new game, sleep would be the last thing on my mind. I had to keep smashing the buttons I was addicted. However, I did get used to it and was able to control my vice. Now in my young adulthood I do play games every now and then. This is how I got introduced to the world computers. Since then, I received a PC when I was in high school to help with my homework. They did not know how much of an impact the PC would have in my life. My parents would have wanted me to study traditional fields such as medicine or to be a lawyer. But there was just something about working on a computer that I decided to have a career in IT. I enrolled at a university where I learned so much about information technologies and computer science. I have gathered a host of skills such as programming/coding, web development, database management, and hardware management. I continue to learn more and advancing these skills further.

Favourite Books

"Reading seldom leads to bad behaviour" - Deborah Harkness

1. The Secret History by Donna Tartt: This book was a trip for me. The Donna is able to capture my imagination completely and takes over and leads you on a journey of self-discovery through a very good story. It was a perfect leisure read.
2. The Richest Man In Babylon by George Clason: The book reveals the biggest secrets in simple words. The author tells simple things on how to lead a good life in general by taking care of your finances first. The author tells a story of Arkad who became the richest man in Babylon through making great decisions and frugal living.
3. Mindset by Dr Carol Dweck: This is the first psychology book I read and it set the bar very high. I wanted to know how to perform at high levels and this book was recommended. The author has done research on why some people seem to do well and others do not. She leads the reader on how they can implement the growth mindset in their lives.
4. Think and Grow Rich by Napoleon Hill: I read this book because I grew up poor and I wanted to understand why some people are poor and others are rich. After reading this book I now know why. The author teaches how one should change their thinking to attract and build wealth.
5. The Ninth House by Leigh Bardugo: A great work of fiction. The author tells a story of Alex who can see ghosts. I've always found fiction to be interesting because of my imagination. This book satisfied that.
